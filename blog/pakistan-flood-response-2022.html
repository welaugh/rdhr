Floods in Pakistan 2022

RDHR Emergency response and support for affected communities

Posted by Aga on October 10, 2022

One of the biggest floods in recent years has put millions of people are at risk of homelessness, hunger and even death. In particular people living in poverty, together with women and children are adversely affected by the crisis. According to UN, there are almost 650,000 pregnant women in the flood-affected areas, with almost 73,000 women expected to deliver in the next month. Many health facilities are partially or fully damaged and gender-based violence is expected to rise.

RDHR is one of the many local organisations who carry out emergency response. Medical assistance, food distribution, shelter provision and WASH are the top priorities of RDHR's intervention. Below are the photos of ongoing activities.

The team RDHR is giving medical assistance to the women and children in the village Khan of Mirpurkhas

Conducting the need assessment in cattle colony IDP camp UMERKOT

RDHR team is in the village Tedi Mori Jhuddo to give mosquito nets to children. Currently, both malaria and dengue are spreading in areas after flood water in Jhuddo Mirpurkhas.
