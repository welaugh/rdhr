Climate burns out women's dream

The global warming brings melancholy without discrimination but already marginalized suffer the most.

Posted by Sajad on March 08, 2020

Climate change has been affecting many economies around the globe. People are not only losing their homes but livelihood as well. Floods, droughts, heatwaves, heavy rainfalls have not only taken many lives but contribute to the displacement of people. The climate change has dire impact on the planet but developing countries have been facing the worst times.

Agriculture employs women

According to the climate change index report, Pakistan is one of the most vulnerable countries to climate change (Burck et al., 2018). Extreme weather changes in the last two decades have rigorously affected lives and livelihoods in the country, especially the marginalized segment of the population who works in the agriculture sector. Agriculture contributes 20, 9 percent in Pakistan’s gross domestic product GDP (Chandio et al, 2015).

The agriculture sector accommodates 70 percent of women out of the total women labor force of Pakistan (Samee et al, 2015). However, only 40% of women find paid work in agriculture and 60% do unpaid and underpaid work at family forms which are 2.6 percent of Pakistan’s total GDP (Abbas, 2018).  But because of climate change, women unemployment in agriculture has been increasing promptly.

Culture alongside with climate

Due to climate change impacts such as water shortage, salinity, heat waves, agriculture production has declined and men are compelled to search for work in industries in nearby cities (Noshriwani, 2016). On the other hand, women are culturally not allowed to go out of the villages to find alternative work. The unemployment in agriculture sector not only affects the overall household economic situation of farmers but it is playing a key role in social and economical exclusion of women.

Women's participation plays a significant role in household income and overall economic growth of an area (Shambaugh, Nunn, and Portman, 2017). To overcome the rural poverty Pakistan needs to take a significant step to help women achieving economic independence (Khaskheli, 2018) but climate change is worsening the economic situation of women in rural areas.

Nothing is impossible, the word itself says I'm possible

To cope up with the challenges of climate change impacts on women it is important to find a new market for their skills where they can participate without worrying about cultural or traditional boundaries. Technology can play a vital role in this regard to connect rural women to urban markets without displacing them. However, it needs a sufficient amount of time, hard work and above all a sincere effort. As Audrey Hepburn says Nothing is impossible, the word itself says I'm possible.
